Radiometer Interference

This project investigates radio frequency interference (RFI) from 5G mmWave systems on NOAA microwave radiometers (MWRs) operating in the 28 GHz band. Using an IBM 28 GHz Phased Array Antenna Module (PAAM), we intentionally transmitted OFDM signals from various azimuth angles toward the NOAA-CESSRST MWR in Manhattan. This setup successfully induced measurable distortions in the MWR’s Level-0 voltage data and final Level-2 weather products, including water vapor profiles.

The PAAM was first characterized in an anechoic chamber before field trials, enabling fine-grained transmit power control. The MWR operates using a two-stage pipeline: Level-0 analog voltage → Level-1 brightness temperature → Level-2 neural network-based atmospheric profiles. Our experiments revealed that the MWR is highly sensitive to sub-dB transmit power changes and azimuthal interference, making even short-range emissions disruptive.

By rotating the PAAM around the MWR, we estimated a partial 60° antenna pattern and defined an interference threshold based on observed degradation in Level-2 outputs. This work is a stepping stone toward creating a full Spectrum Consumption Model (SCM) for dynamic coexistence of 5G and passive sensing systems in urban settings.

Presented as a poster at ACM MobiCom 2024 in Washington, D.C. [Official DOI]
