mmWave Propagation Modeling

Developed around-corner and over-the-top channel models for 28 GHz urban scenarios. Conducted extensive measurements in Manhattan, evaluating path loss and angle of arrival (AoA) for multi-user MIMO (MU-MIMO). Achieved 6.4 dB RMSE with the Street-Clutter-NLOS model, outperforming the 3GPP-UMi-NLOS model by 5 dB. Published in MobiHoc '22 and Infocom '25.

Radiometer Interference

Studied 5G-induced anomalies in atmospheric brightness temperature at 28 GHz, focusing on the impact of 5G mmWave interference on radiometer systems. Published in MobiCom '24.

Backscatter Localization

Designed passive mmWave sensing systems for device-free indoor tracking, leveraging backscatter techniques to localize objects without requiring active devices. This work explores the potential of mmWave for precise indoor sensing applications.

Software-Defined Radar Testbed

Built an SDR-based radar testbed for multi-target tracking using Joint Probabilistic Data Association (JPDA) filtering. Evaluated over-the-air (OTA) and emulated scenarios, including automotive radar applications at sub-6 GHz and 28 GHz. Demonstrated the testbed's capabilities on the Ben Franklin Bridge scenario, showcasing its potential for rapid prototyping and testing of radar systems.

Outdoor-to-Indoor 28 GHz Measurements

Conducted large-scale outdoor-to-indoor (O-I) measurements in Manhattan, analyzing path loss and environmental effects. Developed models showing 20 dB loss from high-loss glass and 5-6 dB from foliage/scaffolding. Achieved 90% coverage with data rates up to 2.8 Gb/s for low-loss glass scenarios, providing insights for mmWave fixed wireless access deployments.
